Karyn Wittmeyer

Hello! I'm a Licensed Mental Health Therapist Associate with a Certificate in Sex Therapy. I have a strong connection to the LGBTQIA+ and non-monogamous communities, but my practice isn’t limited to those populations. I just as much enjoy working with straight and monogamous people. (Don’t worry -- I don't think that all people should be polyamorous, and I'm not here to "convert" you if that's not your preferred relationship style!)

I have additional training in polyvagal theory, attachment theory, CBT and DBT. Translation: I base my practice on the idea that your biology and your history provide context to your values, thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. My job is to help you understand the link between all these things through directed insight and psychoeducation.

Whether you are coming in as an individual, couple, or more, the first question we need to ask is “What change do you want to see in therapy?”

After I get a good idea of the why, what and how of your goals, I will also provide you with a toolkit of skills to help you grow and refine your self-awareness. I will help you practice these skills, which in turn will help you reach your goals. Every few months, we will check in on your progress to see if you are still on track to meet your goals, or if adjustments are needed. We may even find that you’ve reached your goals and are ready to graduation from therapy!

I identify as a non-monogamous pansexual queer woman, and an avid outdoorsman and science nerd. I have spent close to twenty years in positions of leadership, helping build social communities that connect people to each other. I have been a part of the non-monogamy research community for close to a decade. I participated in the American Psychological Association’s Consensual Non-Monogamy Task Force (Division 44), whose goal is to promote wider acceptance of and research into nontraditional relationship configurations in clinical settings. I have conducted preliminary research on metamour relationships that I hope to eventually publish.

In my free time, I enjoy skiing, biking, hiking, backpacking, scuba diving, playing video games, and vacationing with friends and family. I live with two partners, a dog and two cats in a suburban house in Kent. We even have a picket fence.

Branching Out Wellbeing does not work with insurance companies at this time. Costs per session are $120 for individuals and $140 for couples. I have a limited number of sliding scale spots for people who can't afford these fees.
