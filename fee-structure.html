About My Fee Structure

I do not take insurance for many reasons, but I can provide you with a superbill that you can present to your insurance company for reimbursement (referred to as OON – out of network). Many companies are good at reimbursing for therapy, some are not. I’d be happy to talk with you to give you a better idea about which companies are more likely to reimburse you for our work together.

As part of my social justice values, I have created a fee structure that attempts to make therapy affordable for everyone, including me. For each person that signs up for therapy that pays at and over $120 a session, I can open up at least one additional spot for someone who is on a Medicare / Medicaid plan. I have a limited number of spots for Medicare / Medicaid clients at the rate of $30 per session.

I do not require verification of your income. If you would like to have more than two people in the session, the session will be billed under couples' rates, based on combined income of everyone in the session.

Why does couple’s therapy cost more?

Simply put, it’s more work in the same amount of time than with individual therapy. Keeping track of multiple people’s needs and emotional states at the same time is probably more than twice the work of keeping track of a single person. It takes longer for me (and many other therapists) to “recover” from couple’s work than from individual work. It can also be more satisfying and get more done faster than individual work!
