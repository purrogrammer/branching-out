Karyn Wittmeyer

Hi! Finding a new counselor/therapist is never an easy task. It is rife with worry, fear, and a desire to find the right fit with someone. Thank you for taking that first step and considering me as someone to help you navigate your mental health journey.

My theoretical approaches integrate existential therapy, attachment theory, and polyvagal theory. This means that I look at how you answer the big questions in life, how you relate to others, and what has affected you and your body, all through a trauma lens. I operate from an inclusionary, compassionate, strengths-based approach that is body positive, sex positive, anti-racist, and anti-oppression. I use humor regularly in therapy. While it is serious business, that does not necessitate it being somber. Laughter is healing, and can help with relieving stress.I especially love using geeky or gaming terms that engage your creative side to help you level up or face that boss. I view therapy as a collaborative effort. You are the expert on your life. My job is to be a helper and guide on that path as we walk together to aid you in navigating the world you inhabit. I will help you learn new tools for your mental health toolbox, and co-create ways of using them that may not be obvious. I work a lot with metaphor and analogies, tailoring them to each person to help convey meaning, and to make sure we have similar understandings of what is being discussed.

I have training and experience in attachment theory, polyvagal theory, healing within oppressive systems, working with different forms of trauma, CBT, and DBT. I greatly enjoy working with over 21, LGBTQIA+ folx, those in any of the types of relationships under the umbrella term of consensual non-monogamy, gender identity exploration, sexual identity exploration, kink and kink dynamics, sex workers, differently abled (neurodiverse, physical disability, & chronically ill/spoonies), and any or all intersections of the above. I work with individuals, couples, and moresomes (on an as needed basis). I love helping clients to find feelings of safety in their bodies, and enhance or develop the ability to thrive. Not all clients may desire this, which is completely okay!

While I specialize in working with the above populations.

My identities include being queer, Jewish, disabled, non-monogamous, pansexual, and genderfluid. In my personal life I love spending time with my pup, taking care of my home, gardens, and many many plants, spending time with my partner, metas, and friends, playing D&D, board games, or video games, partner dancing, travel (when it is safe), and cooking a myriad of tasty treats.

I am a Licensed Mental Health Counselor Associate (License # 61198136) in the State of Washington. This means that I have recently finished my studies and am working on full licensure. I am also working towards credentialing with the American Association of Sexuality Educators, Counselors, and Therapists (AASECT). I completed a Masters of Arts in Clinical Mental Health Counseling and a Certificate in Sex Therapy from Antioch University Seattle.
